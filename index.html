What we heard

Your most expensive hidden cost is everything that happens between brief and signed estimate.

You have a beautiful Koregaon Park studio, 14 designers, 2 project managers, 12 live projects across residential, commercial and one hospitality build. What you do not have is a system that turns a brief into a sign-off-ready proposal, holds the moodboard somewhere a client can actually find it, and gets site-visit photos off a designer's phone before they fade. So every new enquiry restarts the same 4-hour cycle, and the WhatsApp PDF you sent on Tuesday is gone by Friday.

Here is what you are dealing with

The 4-hour tax, the lost moodboard, the photo on someone's phone.

  • 4 to 5 hours per client proposal, assembled by hand across 7 to 8 Excel tabs - profile, scope, BOQ, costing, payment plan, terms, references. Done by you or a senior designer, because nobody else holds the pricing logic in their head.
  • Industry conversion on mid-to-high-ticket residential proposals is 20% to 30%. Across 12 active projects and a typical pipeline of 30 to 40 enquiries a quarter, roughly 70% of that proposal time is effectively unpaid.
  • Every revision round costs another 60 to 90 minutes - open the workbook, change rates, re-version the tabs, re-send. Two to three revisions is normal. That is 3 to 4 hours of senior time per closed project, on revisions alone.
  • Client moodboards are handed off as WhatsApp PDFs that get lost in 48 hours. The client comes back asking "can you resend that Italian marble reference?" and someone has to rebuild it from scratch.
  • Site-visit photos and voice notes sit on the designer's phone for 2 to 4 days before they reach the studio. By then the moment, the question, the on-site decision is gone.
  • Behind that, the studio's full operating stack: a 7 to 8 sheet workbook per client, a 14-designer team plus 2 managers and a 4-person execution crew, reporting verbally at 2pm and 4pm. No audit trail beyond what anyone remembers.
Here is what you asked for

AI that drafts in your voice, a moodboard the client cannot lose, a feed the site team posts straight into.

  • AI-drafted proposals from your approved rate library, in your language, in your format. You review and sign off - never auto-sent to a client.
  • A Showcase Gallery that turns every proposal into a live mini-website - moodboard images, material swatches, project-by-project sections, a one-tap "Call Anjali" button right inside. Replaces the WhatsApp PDF that disappears.
  • A Site-Visit Voice Drop - designer at the site speaks into the phone, the transcript auto-tags by project and lands in the studio feed before they are back in Koregaon Park.
  • A Client Sign-Off Portal - clients tap "approve" on the kitchen, "revisit" on the master bedroom, section by section. No more "did they actually read the whole PDF?"
  • A 3D Moodboard Pinboard - drag any Pinterest, Instagram or Behance reference straight into the right project's mood file. Auto-attributed, searchable, never lost.
  • GST-aware from day one with SAC 998391 for design, SAC 9954 for site execution, HSN 9403 for furniture. Clean input for your CA.
  • No lock-in. Weekly Excel + image archive export to your Google Drive, forever.

What happens if nothing changes.

Two costs keep compounding. First, every unconverted proposal is 4 hours of senior time you will not get back, and the studio's pipeline only grows as Koregaon Park and Kalyani Nagar referrals stack up. Second, from FY 2024-25 onwards, the Ministry of Corporate Affairs requires every company using accounting software to maintain an audit trail that is tamper-proof and retained for 8 years (MCA Rule 3, Companies Amendment Rules 2022). Spreadsheets and WhatsApp PDFs do not produce that trail. You are one audit away from reconstructing 9 years of project-level inflows, outflows, BOQ revisions and client sign-offs from memory.

What could go wrong, and how we have pre-handled it

The five risks we flag before you do.

Every engagement at this scale has predictable failure modes. Naming them up front is how we keep the conversation honest.

You keep running Excel in parallel and never fully migrate.
Mitigation. Two built-in safety nets. First, you can start at Proposal Engine (₹1,75,000) or Pilot (₹75,000) instead of the full Command Centre - both are designed so you can stop there if that is all you actually use. Second, if you do take the full Command Centre, the week-6 adoption KPI is written into the SOW: fewer than 40% of new projects in-system triggers a 30% refund and a pivot back to the Proposal Engine SKU. Your 2 hours per week of UAT time is a signed clause.
The 14-designer team drops the system in month 3.
Mitigation. Training videos in Hindi and Marathi, not just English. We onboard the 2 managers first (hardest converters), the 4-person execution crew second through the Site-Visit Voice Drop alone, designers third. Excel + moodboard archive export runs weekly as a fallback, so the team never feels cornered.
Scope creeps via verbal asks on site or in the Koregaon Park studio.
Mitigation. Change requests only honoured in writing, priced at ₹3,000 per hour with a written impact estimate you approve before any work begins. No verbal CR is ever actioned. This clause is in the SOW signature page.
AI-drafted proposal or BOQ hallucinates and damages client trust.
Mitigation. Three-column output (AI draft, your historical pattern, final). Confidence score shown on every draft. Quantity deviations from historical patterns flagged when they exceed 10%. Mandatory human sign-off. The Showcase Gallery itself does not go live to the client until you press send. No client ever receives an AI draft un-reviewed.
Monthly fee starts to feel like wasted spend in month 4.
Mitigation. Prepaid SKU (₹7,80,000) bundles year one monthly into setup, so there is no month 4 invoice. Year two is framed as Compliance, Hosting and Continuity - the 8-year MCA audit trail, the live Showcase Gallery hosting and the moodboard archive are the anchor value, not "maintenance".
